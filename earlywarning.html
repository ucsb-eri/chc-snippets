Early Warning Initiatives

The Climate Hazards Center (CHC) supports early warning initiatives to monitor and forecast climate extremes. Below are some of our active projects that provide actionable information for vulnerable communities.

Kenya Subseasonal Forecast Update

This page hosts rainfall monitoring and forecast results co-developed by the Kenya Meteorological Department, Famine Early Warning System, the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology, the UCAR Innovative Capacity Development Program and the Rhiza-led Nimbus program.

Kenya Rainfall Monitor

The Rainfall Monitor shows snippets of recent rainfall conditions in Kenya using a combination of CHIRPS and CHIRPS-GEFS forecasts. Snippets are a publicly accessible tool to display maps from the Climate Hazards Center Early Warning Explorer (EWX) online application on your own webpage.

Madagascar Rainfall Monitor

The Rainfall Monitor shows snippets of recent rainfall conditions in Madagascar using a combination of CHIRPS and CHIRPS-GEFS forecasts. Snippets are a publicly accessible tool to display maps from the Climate Hazards Center Early Warning Explorer (EWX) online application on your own webpage.

Kakuma Refugee Camp Heat Tracker

This project monitors extreme heat events in Kakuma Refugee Camp using satellite and ground-based temperature data. The Heat Tracker provides near real-time alerts to humanitarian agencies and camp managers, helping reduce heat-related risks.
